History

The temple, at a height of 1,585 metres (5,200 ft) is 12 km from Katra on Trikuta hill. It is about 61 km from Jammu city.[10][11] A geological study of the Holy Cave has indicated its age to be nearly a million years. There is also a mention of the Trikuta hill in Rigveda, the place where the temple is located.[12] The Mahabharata, which gives the account of the Pandavas and the Kurukshetra War, does mention the worship of goddess Vaishno Devi. Before the Kurukshetra War Arjuna is said to have worshipped Durga by the advice of Lord Krishna for the blessings. Pleased by his devotion, Mother Goddess appeared in front of him in the form of Vaishno Devi. When goddess appeared, Arjuna started praising her with a stotra, in which a Shloka goes by saying ‘ Jambookatak Chityaishu Nityam Sannihitalaye ’, which means ‘you who always dwell in the temple on the slope of the mountain in Jambhu’ — probably referring to the present day Jammu.[13] Former Governor of Jammu and Kashmir Jagmohan says, "the Mata Vaishno Devi shrine is an ancient one whose antiquity is pre-Mahabharathan, Lord Krishna is believed to have advised Arjuna to go up in the hills of 'Jambhu' and seek the blessings of Vaishno Devi before taking up arms in the battlefield. 'Jambhu' is identified with present-day Jammu. Arjuna while worshipping Vaishno Devi, calls Her, the highest Yogin who is free from decrepitude and decay, who is the Mother of the Vedas and the Science of Vedanta and who is giver of Victory and personification of victory itself".

Significance of Vaishnodevi Temple

Mata Vaishnodevi Temple has three significant stone structures called Pindies as presiding deity, which symbolizes Mother Goddess in her three divine forms - Saraswati, Lakshmi and Kali. Mother Kali bestows her devotees with confidence and strength to battle through life. Goddess Lakshmi blesses one with prosperity, good fortune and well-being. Goddess Sarawati imparts divine wisdom and true knowledge. This epitomizes the three divine characteristics of Mother Shakti – Creation, Preservation and Destruction. The striking balance of these three forces marks the significance of this Holy Shrine.

Places to Visit around Vaishno Devi Katra

Vaishno Devi, located in Katra, Jammu, and Kashmir, is a popular pilgrimage site for Hindus. Here are some places to visit in and around Vaishno Devi Katra
Vaishno Devi Temple

The main attraction, the Vaishno Devi Temple is dedicated to Goddess Vaishno Devi. Pilgrims trek uphill to reach the temple, which is the Trikuta Mountains.

Ardh Kuwari Cave

Located midway on the trek to Vaishno Devi Temple, this cave is believed to be the place where Goddess Vaishno Devi meditated for nine months.

Bhairavnath Temple

Visiting the Vaishno Devi Temple, pilgrims usually visit the Bhairavnath Temple, dedicated to Bhairav Nath, believed to be the guardian deity of Vaishno Devi.

Sanjichhat

It is a popular stopover point during the trek. From here, one can get a breathtaking view of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Ban Ganga

This is the place where Mata Vaishno Devi is believed to have taken a bath. Pilgrims often take a dip in the holy waters of Ban Ganga.

Charan Paduka

This place is marked by foot imprints of Goddess Vaishno Devi. It's believed that the Goddess rested here for some time during her journey.

Himkoti

Situated around 2.5 km from Vaishno Devi, Himkoti offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. It's a great place to relax and enjoy.

Jhajjar Kotli

It's a scenic picnic spot located around 15 km from Katra. The place is famous for its natural beauty and waterfalls.

Festivals and Celebrations

Among the festivals celebrated at Vaishno Devi Mandir in Katra.

Navaratri

Navaratri holds the highest significance at Vaishno Devi Mandir, spanning nine days and nights of dedicated worship to the nine forms of Goddess Durga. It is the most important festival due to its extensive duration and the large number of devotees it attracts.

Diwali

Diwali, the festival of lights, is another crucial celebration at the temple. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. Devotees from far and wide gather to light lamps and offer prayers to Goddess Vaishno Devi.

Sharad Navaratri

Sharad Navaratri, occurring in September or October, is celebrated with great fervor and devotion. It is marked by various rituals, prayers, and offerings to the Goddess, making it one of the significant festivals at the temple.

Maha Shivaratri

Maha Shivaratri, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is observed with reverence at Vaishno Devi Mandir. Devotees fast, meditate, and offer prayers to seek blessings from Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati.

Vaishno Devi Jayanti

Vaishno Devi Jayanti, commemorating the birth anniversary of Goddess Vaishno Devi, is celebrated with special prayers, rituals, and devotional songs. While it holds immense importance, it ranks slightly lower due to its single-day observance compared to the longer festivals like Navaratri.
